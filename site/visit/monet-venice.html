A Gallery Companion

Monet and Venice

de Young Museum · Herbst Galleries · through July 26, 2026

In the autumn of 1908, a sixty-eight-year-old who had spent decades insisting Venice was too beautiful to be painted finally got on the train — and painted it thirty-seven times. More of those canvases hang together downstairs at the de Young than at any time since 1912. This companion walks the show's rooms in order: eighteen short stops in the docent's voice, each about a minute — listen as you arrive at each room, and let the paintings find you. Every stop also carries its text, for quiet galleries and second readings.
